# Projection Strategy for the USA LiDAR Dataset

A complete guide to choosing target coordinate reference systems (CRSs) for the
LiDAR projects in [`laz-by-project.csv`](./laz-by-project.csv) — covering the
concepts, the trade-offs, the concrete numbers, and a ready-to-run per-project
reprojection plan.

**The single most important idea:** there is no one "best" projection — the
right choice depends on what you're doing with the data. Unifying everything for
storage/analysis and minimizing distortion for engineering drawings pull in
**opposite** directions.

---

## 0. TL;DR

| Goal | Horizontal CRS | Vertical CRS | Why |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Merged archive / tiling / area stats** | `EPSG:6350` — NAD83(2011) / Conus Albers (m) | `EPSG:5703` — NAVD88 height (m) | One equal-area grid spanning all zones & both states |
| **France-style single drawable grid** | custom conformal Lambert (see §6.3) | `EPSG:5703` | One grid, conformal, ~2,300 ppm, drawable |
| **One common drawable grid per state** | custom conformal Lambert, FL & GA separately (see §4.2) | `EPSG:5703` | Conformal, ~600–850 ppm, drawable; custom (no EPSG) |
| **Engineering drawings, least distortion** | per-project State Plane zone (m) or custom LDP | `EPSG:5703` | Conformal, ≤100 ppm (LDP: ~5 ppm); State Plane is standard EPSG |

A concrete per-project plan for the engineering case is generated by
[`make_reprojection_plan.py`](./make_reprojection_plan.py) → see §7.

## 2. Concepts you need (self-contained reference)

### 2.1 The fundamental trade-off: conformal vs equal-area

A flat map of a curved Earth must distort *something*. The two properties that
matter here cannot both be kept at once:

- **Conformal** = preserves **angles and local shapes**. Right angles stay
  right; bearings are correct; a small square stays square. Areas are *not*
  preserved (far regions look too big).
- **Equal-area** = preserves **area**. To do so it deliberately stretches one
  direction and shrinks the perpendicular one, so shapes shear and angles are
  wrong.

> **Rule of thumb:** conformal = right shapes/angles, wrong areas;
> equal-area = right areas, wrong shapes/angles. **Engineering drawings need
> conformal.**

### 2.2 What "conformal" means in detail

At any point, a conformal projection scales distance by the **same factor in
every direction** (the scale is *isotropic* locally). Consequences:

- angles measured on the map equal angles on the ground;
- small shapes are preserved (circles stay circles);
- the scale factor still *changes from place to place* — conformal does **not**
  mean zero distortion, it means the distortion at each point is the same in all
  directions, so it can be corrected with a single per-location number.

We measured this as **anisotropy** (E-W scale minus N-S scale). Near-zero
anisotropy ⇒ conformal.

### 2.3 Linear distortion and "ppm"

**ppm = parts per million.** 100 ppm = 0.0001 = 0.01% = a ratio of 1:10,000. In
map terms it is how much a measured distance differs from the true ground
distance — a **scale (multiplicative) error that grows with length:**

| Ground distance | at 100 ppm (State Plane) | at 5 ppm (good LDP) | at 12,000 ppm (Albers, S. FL) |
|---|---|---|---|
| 100 m | 10 mm | 0.5 mm | 1.2 m |
| 1 km | 100 mm | 5 mm | 12 m |
| 10 km | 1 m | 5 cm | 120 m |

### 2.4 The combined scale factor

Real-world distortion for survey/engineering has **two** parts:

1. **Grid distortion** — the projection's own scale error (the ppm above).
2. **Elevation reduction** — grid coordinates sit on the ellipsoid, but the
   ground is higher; a point at height *h* needs a factor ≈ *h*/R (R ≈ 6,371 km),
   about −157 ppm per 1,000 m of elevation.

A **Low Distortion Projection (LDP)** is designed to cancel *both* at a specific
site. State Plane only addresses the grid part. (In Florida, near sea level, the
elevation part is tiny; in the Georgia mountains it matters more.)

## 3. Choosing a target CRS — by goal

### 3.1 Goal A — a unified archive

For one merged dataset / COPC tiling / cross-state area statistics:

- **Horizontal: `EPSG:6350` (NAD83(2011) / Conus Albers, metres).** One
  CONUS-wide projection covers both UTM zones, all State Plane zones, and both
  states in a single consistent frame. Already used by 18 projects;
  standardizes everything on NAD83(2011). It is **equal-area** — perfect for
  storage, visualization, and area stats; **wrong for drawings** (see §5).
- **Vertical: `EPSG:5703` (NAVD88 height, metres).**

There is no standard compound EPSG for Albers+NAVD88; set them separately, e.g.
PDAL `out_srs="EPSG:6350+5703"`.

### 3.2 Goal B — engineering drawings (least distortion)

Distortion is **local**, and drawings require a **conformal** projection. This
rules out Albers (equal-area) and UTM (k₀ = 0.9996 → up to ~400 ppm). Two tiers:

**B1 — Florida/Georgia State Plane, metric (conformal, ≤ ~100 ppm).** Pick the
zone per project; do not merge zones. Prefer NAD83(2011). See §6 for the codes.

**B2 — custom Low Distortion Projection per site (conformal, ~5 ppm).** A
Transverse Mercator centred on the site with the scale factor set for its ground
elevation:

```
+proj=tmerc +lat_0=<site_lat> +lon_0=<site_lon> +k=1.0000xxx +x_0=50000 +y_0=0 +datum=NAD83 +units=m +no_defs
```

with `k ≈ 1 + elevation/R`. Drives combined distortion to a few ppm.

## 4. Metric EPSG reference (all verified with `projinfo`)

The dataset is mostly in **US-survey-foot** State Plane codes; their exact
**metric** twins (identical projection geometry, metre axis units) are:

| Zone | NAD83(2011) — recommended | NAD83 (older) | ftUS (as in the data) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Florida East | `EPSG:6437` | `EPSG:26958` | 2236 / 6438 |
| Florida North | `EPSG:6440` | `EPSG:26960` | 2238 / 6441 |
| Florida West | `EPSG:6442` | `EPSG:26959` | 2237 |
| Georgia East | `EPSG:6444` | `EPSG:26966` | 6445 |
| Georgia West | `EPSG:6446` | `EPSG:26967` | 6447 |

Vertical: **`EPSG:5703`** = NAVD88 height (metres); `EPSG:6360` is the ftUS twin.

> Gotcha discovered along the way: code numbering is not regular — e.g. `6443` is
> *Georgia West (ftUS)*, not the metric West; the metric West is `6446`. Always
> verify the axis unit, don't infer from the number.

### 4.1 Recommended: Florida's 3 standard conformal zones (metric)

For Florida, the best choice that is **conformal, metric, standard (has an EPSG
code), and low-distortion** is the official **Florida State Plane** system. It
covers the state in **three zones** — and that 3-zone split is a feature, not a
compromise: it is exactly what holds distortion to ≤100 ppm and matches the
state's L-shape (two N-S peninsula zones + one E-W panhandle zone). No custom
projection, no WKT to carry — every consumer recognizes the code.

Use the **NAD83(2011), metre** codes:

| Zone | EPSG | Method (all conformal) | Origin | Distortion | Covers (counties / cities) |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Florida East** | `6437` | Transverse Mercator | CM −81°, k₀ 0.99994 | ≤100 ppm | E peninsula — Jacksonville, Orlando, Miami, the Keys, Collier |
| **Florida West** | `6442` | Transverse Mercator | CM −82°, k₀ 0.99994 | ≤100 ppm | Gulf peninsula — Tampa, St. Pete, Sarasota, Fort Myers |
| **Florida North** | `6440` | Lambert Conic Conformal (2SP) | SPs 29.583°/30.75° | ≤100 ppm | Panhandle + north tier — Tallahassee, Pensacola, Gainesville |

- **Why two methods?** The peninsula zones are **Transverse Mercator** (best for
  N-S extent); the panhandle zone is **Lambert Conic Conformal** (best for E-W
  extent). Both are conformal, so all three preserve angles/shapes — drawable.
- **Vertical:** pair any zone with **`EPSG:5703`** (NAVD88 height, metres), e.g.
  `out_srs="EPSG:6437+5703"`.
- **Georgia** uses the same idea with two zones — **`EPSG:6444`** (Georgia East)
  and **`EPSG:6446`** (Georgia West), both Transverse Mercator, metric.

**Don't substitute UTM.** UTM zones 16N/17N (`EPSG:6345`/`6346`) are also
standard, conformal, and metric, but a worse fit: k₀ = 0.9996 allows up to
~400 ppm (4× State Plane), and the zone boundary at −84° slices through the
panhandle, whereas State Plane's North zone keeps it whole.

**Bottom line:** for "conformal + metric + standard," reproject each project to
its Florida State Plane zone (`6437`/`6440`/`6442`) + NAVD88 m. This is precisely
what [`make_reprojection_plan.py`](./make_reprojection_plan.py) does (§7), and it
needs no custom projection — prefer it over the custom per-state Lambert (§4.2)
unless you specifically require a single grid per state.

### 4.2 Alternative: one custom Lambert per state (single grid per state)

If you specifically want **one seamless coordinate space per state** — a single
grid for all of Florida and a single grid for all of Georgia, rather than the
2–3 State Plane zones — use one **custom Lambert Conformal Conic per state**.
This is the "1 zone/state" solution: still conformal, still metric, drawable,
but with somewhat more distortion than the zoned standard, and (unavoidably)
**custom** — no EPSG code.

Definitions (vertical stays `EPSG:5703` for both):

```
# Florida  (std parallels 25.5°/30°)   worst distortion ~850 ppm
+proj=lcc +lat_1=25.5 +lat_2=30 +lat_0=27.75 +lon_0=-84 +x_0=0 +y_0=0 +datum=NAD83 +units=m +no_defs

# Georgia  (std parallels 31°/34°)     worst distortion ~615 ppm
+proj=lcc +lat_1=31 +lat_2=34 +lat_0=32.5 +lon_0=-83.3 +x_0=0 +y_0=0 +datum=NAD83 +units=m +no_defs
```

| | 3 standard zones (§4.1) | 1 custom Lambert / state (this) |
|---|---|---|
| Zones per state | FL 3, GA 2 | **1 each** |
| Worst distortion | ≤100 ppm | FL ~850, GA ~615 ppm |
| Conformal? | yes | yes |
| Metric? | yes | yes |
| Standard EPSG code? | **yes** | **no — custom WKT/PROJ** |
| Ties to survey/county data | yes | needs reprojection |

**Use it when** a single statewide tiling / coordinate space is the priority and
you accept carrying the WKT with the data. **Otherwise prefer §4.1** — the zoned
standard is lower-distortion *and* interoperable. See §6.4 for the full
derivation, the LCC-not-TM reasoning (Florida's panhandle), and the
standard-vs-custom trade-off in detail.

## 5. Why Albers (6350) is wrong for engineering drawings — quantified

Conus Albers is **equal-area**, so it distorts scale and shape, and the
distortion is **anisotropic** (different E-W vs N-S). Computed analytically and
confirmed against `proj` (agreement to 5 decimals):

| Location | lat | E-W scale | N-S scale | Max linear | Angular error |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| FL Keys | 24.5° | 1.0139 | 0.9863 | **1.39 % (13,900 ppm)** | 1.6° |
| S Florida | 25° | 1.0123 | 0.9879 | 1.23 % | 1.4° |
| Central FL | 27° | 1.0063 | 0.9938 | 0.63 % | 0.7° |
| (std parallel) | 29.5° | 1.0000 | 1.0000 | 0 % | 0° |
| N FL / S GA | 31° | 0.9969 | 1.0031 | 0.31 % (best) | 0.35° |
| N Georgia | 34° | 0.9925 | 1.0076 | 0.76 % | 0.86° |
| N Georgia | 35° | 0.9915 | 1.0086 | 0.86 % | 0.98° |

Empirical check @ lat 25, 1 km segments: E-W 1000 m ground → **1012.22 m** grid;
N-S 1000 m ground → **987.94 m** grid.

**Impact on a drawing:**

- **Distances are wrong and unfixable by one factor.** At the Keys an E-W 100 m
  run plots as 101.4 m but an N-S 100 m run as 98.6 m. Because the error depends
  on *direction*, the standard single combined-scale-factor correction cannot
  work.
- **Right angles aren't right; shapes shear.** A 100 m × 100 m square plots as
  ~101.4 × 98.6 m. Bearings off by up to **~1.6°**.
- **Areas look right while lengths don't** — a trap: equal-area keeps the area
  takeoff correct while every dimension feeding it is wrong.
- **Mismatch with the legal/survey frame.** Surveyors, plats, and county GIS are
  in State Plane feet; an Albers drawing won't tie to control or overlay, and
  grid-north differs.

Albers is the *right* tool for the merged archive (§3.1); the downsides only
bite when you take dimensions/angles/coordinates off the map.

## 6. The France comparison — why one national grid can work

France distributes IGN **LiDAR HD** in **RGF93 / Lambert-93 (EPSG:2154)** +
**NGF-IGN69 (EPSG:5720)** — one horizontal grid and one vertical datum for the
whole country. France spans ~42–51° N (~9° of latitude) — almost the same span
as this FL+GA dataset (~10.5°). So why does *one* projection work there?

### 6.1 Because Lambert-93 is conformal, not because it's low-distortion

| System | Type | Max linear distortion | Anisotropy | Drawable? |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **US Albers 6350** (FL @25°) | equal-area | ~12,000 ppm | **~24,000 ppm** | ❌ |
| **France Lambert-93** (whole country) | conformal | ~2,200 ppm (@51°) | **~5 ppm** | ✅ |
| France **CC zones** 3942–3950 | conformal | ~50–85 ppm | ~0 | ✅ precision |
| US **State Plane** | conformal | ≤100 ppm | ~0 | ✅ precision |

Measured Lambert-93 scale (via `proj`): 1448 ppm @42.5°, ~3 ppm @44°/49° (the
standard parallels), 949 ppm @46.5° (middle), 2201 ppm @51° — **anisotropy only
3–6 ppm everywhere.** That ~0 anisotropy is the whole point: a ~2 m/km scale
error that is the *same in every direction* is correctable with one per-location
factor, and shapes/angles stay right. Albers' 24,000 ppm anisotropy is not
correctable and shears everything.

> Note: Lambert-93's raw scale distortion (~2,200 ppm) is *comparable to or
> larger than* Albers in magnitude — yet it's drawable and Albers isn't. The
> deciding property is conformality, not the size of the scale error.

### 6.2 France runs the same two-tier system as the US

| Tier | France | USA |
|---|---|---|
| One grid for distribution/general use | Lambert-93 (**conformal**) | Albers 6350 (**equal-area**) |
| Local low-distortion for precise work | CC zones 3942–3950 (~80 ppm) | State Plane / LDP (≤100 ppm) |

The only real difference: France's *national* tier is conformal, so it's usable
for everyday engineering; the US national equal-area tier is not. France still
drops to CC zones for cadastre — exactly as you'd drop to State Plane/LDP.

### 6.3 The France-style option for this dataset

If you want France's "one grid for everything" *and* drawability, the honest
equivalent is **not Albers — it's a single conformal Lambert for FL+GA**:

```
+proj=lcc +lat_1=26.25 +lat_2=33.25 +lat_0=29.75 +lon_0=-83.5 +datum=NAD83 +units=m +no_defs
```

Computed distortion: 2,300 ppm @24.5°, 708 ppm @27°, 1,865 ppm @29.75°,
1,104 ppm @32°, 2,383 ppm @35° — **~0 anisotropy**, i.e. essentially identical
behaviour to Lambert-93 over France, and drawable with a per-site scale factor.

**Why the US uses zones instead:** this works only because the data is two
adjacent states (~1,200 km). A single conformal conic for *all* of CONUS
(~4,500 km wide) would far exceed Lambert-93's distortion — the historical
reason the US adopted zoned State Plane rather than one national grid.

### 6.4 The better single-grid choice: one conformal Lambert per state

Since the data is two states, the natural unit for a *single common drawable
grid* is **per state**, not one grid for both. Narrowing each projection to one
state's latitude band cuts the distortion ~3–4× versus the combined grid in
§6.3, while still giving exactly **one coordinate space per state**:

| Scope | Std parallels | Worst distortion |
|---|---|---|
| One Lambert for FL+GA together (§6.3) | 26.25°/33.25° | ~2,380 ppm (2.4 m/km) |
| **One Lambert for Florida** | 25.5°/30° | **~850 ppm (0.85 m/km)** |
| **One Lambert for Georgia** | 31°/34° | **~615 ppm (0.62 m/km)** |

Per-latitude detail (k = scale factor, distortion = |k−1|):

```
FLORIDA   lat 24.5 k=1.000831 (831 ppm)   lat 26 (303)   lat 27.75 (771)   lat 29 (535)   lat 31 (847)
GEORGIA   lat 30.3 k=1.000392 (392 ppm)   lat 31.5 (190) lat 32.5 (343)    lat 33.5 (191) lat 35 (615)
```

Definitions (vertical stays `EPSG:5703` for both):

```
# Florida
+proj=lcc +lat_1=25.5 +lat_2=30 +lat_0=27.75 +lon_0=-84 +x_0=0 +y_0=0 +datum=NAD83 +units=m +no_defs
# Georgia
+proj=lcc +lat_1=31 +lat_2=34 +lat_0=32.5 +lon_0=-83.3 +x_0=0 +y_0=0 +datum=NAD83 +units=m +no_defs
```

**Use Lambert (LCC), not Transverse Mercator, for a statewide grid.** Florida is
L-shaped — a N-S peninsula plus an E-W panhandle (the very reason official FL
State Plane needs three zones). A single Transverse Mercator would blow up across
the panhandle (TM distortion grows with E-W distance from its central meridian —
~1,900+ ppm at the panhandle's ~4° spread). A Lambert conic's distortion depends
**only on latitude**, so it ignores the E-W extent and absorbs the panhandle
gracefully — that's why the FL figure stays ~850 ppm.

#### This is a custom projection — the trade-off

The per-state Lambert (like the LDP) is a **custom projection: a hand-defined
PROJ string with no EPSG code.** That is unavoidable for "one drawable grid per
state," because no standard EPSG offers it — the standard single-statewide code
(**`EPSG:3086`, NAD83 / Florida GDL Albers**) is *equal-area* (same shape/angle
problem as Conus Albers; fine for GIS, wrong for drawings), and the standard
*conformal* system (State Plane) is deliberately split into zones. You can have
standard-and-conformal **or** single-grid-per-state, not both.

Consequences of going custom:

- **No EPSG code to communicate** — you must ship the full WKT/PROJ definition
  with the data, not just a code.
- **The definition must travel with every file.** LAS/LAZ/COPC can embed a full
  WKT CRS in the header, so it is technically fine, but every consumer (CAD, GIS,
  a surveyor) must read WKT, not just look up a code.
- **Mismatch risk** if the WKT is stripped or re-tagged — there is no registry to
  recover the exact projection.
- **Won't tie to survey/county data**, which is in State Plane, without
  reprojection.

**When to pick it:** only if you genuinely want a single seamless coordinate
space per state (e.g. one statewide tiling). For ordinary engineering drawings —
which are local — the per-zone **State Plane** option (§3.2, §7) is conformal,
lower distortion (≤100 ppm), *and* a standard EPSG code, so it is usually the
better choice despite using more than one zone.

## 7. Per-project reprojection plan (engineering case)

[`make_reprojection_plan.py`](./make_reprojection_plan.py) assigns every project
to its correct **metric NAD83(2011) State Plane zone** + NAVD88 metres, and
emits:

- [`reprojection-plan.csv`](./reprojection-plan.csv) — one row per project:
  source H/V, assigned zone, target H/V, full `out_srs`, vertical flag.
- `reproject.sh` — a `pdal translate ... reprojection` command per project.
  Usage: `reproject.sh <in.laz> <out.laz> <PROJECT>`.

Regenerate: `python3 make_reprojection_plan.py`.

### How the zone is chosen

- Projects already tagged with a Florida State Plane zone **keep that zone**
  (just converted to metric/NAD83(2011)).
- UTM, Conus Albers, and Georgia projects have their zone **inferred from the
  centroid**: Georgia split at lon −83.2° (East/West); Florida North if
  lat ≥ 29.5° and lon ≤ −82.0° (panhandle/north), else West if lon ≤ −81.7°
  (gulf), else East (atlantic).
- Boundaries are approximate (real SPCS zones follow county lines) but always
  land on a conformal zone whose central meridian is near the data, so
  distortion stays low even near a boundary. Spot-checked, e.g.
  `FL_Southwest_B` → East is correct (Collier County is officially FL East).

### Result

| Target zone | EPSG (metric) | Projects |
|---|---|---|
| Florida East | `6437` | 39 |
| Florida North | `6440` | 27 |
| Florida West | `6442` | 16 |
| Georgia East | `6444` | 4 |
| Georgia West | `6446` | 3 |

### Projects needing manual vertical confirmation (9)

Missing or invalid vertical code — `reproject.sh` reprojects **horizontal only**
for these (Z passes through untouched). Confirm the source vertical datum **and
units** before adding `+5703`:

`FL_HurricaneMichael_6_2020`, `FL_PalmBeachCo_2016`, `FL_Panhandle_B1_2018`,
`FL_Panhandle_B2_2018`, `FL_Panhandle_TL_2018`, `FL_PeaceRiver_2014`,
`FL_Peninsular_FDEM_Orange_2018`, `FL_Suwannee_NCentral_2014`,
`USGS_LPC_FL_Panhandle_B3_2018_LAS_2019`.

## 8. Caveats and open items

- **Datum shift NAD83 → NAD83(2011)** is a real ~few-cm transform — ensure the
  pipeline performs it, not just relabels.
- **Geoid models** differ (mostly Geoid12B, a few Geoid18). Changing the
  horizontal datum does not require re-gridding the geoid; keep Geoid12B unless
  you deliberately want 18 (re-gridding *is* a real vertical transform).
- **The 9 flagged vertical projects** must be resolved before trusting Z.
- **Units** — confirm metre vs ftUS against your CAD/deliverable target;
  everything recommended here is metric.
- **LDP tier** needs a per-project ground elevation (not in the CSV) to set the
  scale factor; can be added if few-ppm accuracy is required.

## Appendix — how the distortion numbers were produced (reproducible)

- **Albers / Lambert-93 / CC / custom LCC scale factors:** computed two ways and
  cross-checked — (a) analytic spherical scale-factor formulas (Albers:
  `k_parallel = sqrt(C − 2n·sinφ)/cosφ`, h = 1/k; Lambert: conformal LCC scale),
  and (b) empirically with PROJ: transform a point and a point 1 km away
  (geodesic, via `geod +ellps=GRS80`) and compare grid distance (via
  `cs2cs EPSG:4326 <target>`). Analytic and empirical agreed to 5 decimals.
- **EPSG codes / axis units:** verified with `projinfo EPSG:<code>`.
- **Zone assignment & flags:** `make_reprojection_plan.py` (centroids from the
  WKT geometry in `laz-by-project.csv`).
